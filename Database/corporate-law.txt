### ACT
TITLE: The Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008
TYPE: Unspecified
DESCRIPTION: |
  This Act introduced and governs the formation and regulation of Limited Liability Partnerships (LLPs). An LLP is a hybrid corporate structure that combines the flexibility of a partnership with the benefit of limited liability of a company.
KEY_PROVISIONS: |
  Body Corporate and Separate Legal Entity (Section 3): An LLP is a body corporate and a legal entity separate from its partners, with perpetual succession.
TERMINOLOGY:
  - Limited Liability Partnership (LLP) :: A body corporate formed and incorporated under the Act, which is a legal entity separate from its partners.
  - Partner :: Any person who becomes a partner in the LLP in accordance with the LLP agreement.
  - Designated Partner :: A partner who is designated as such in the incorporation document and is responsible for compliance with the Act's provisions.
  - LLP Agreement :: A written agreement between the partners of the LLP that determines their mutual rights and duties.
