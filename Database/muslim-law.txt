### ACT
TITLE: The Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937
TYPE: Unspecified
DESCRIPTION: |
  This Act makes the Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) applicable to Muslims in India for various personal matters. Its primary objective was to abrogate customs and usages that were contrary to the principles of Islamic law and to ensure that Shariat governs personal disputes among Muslims.
KEY_PROVISIONS: |
  Application of Personal Law to Muslims (Section 2): Mandates that in all questions (except those relating to agricultural land) regarding intestate succession, special property of females, marriage, dissolution of marriage (including talaq, ila, zihar, lian, khula, and mubara'at), maintenance, dower, guardianship, gifts, trusts, and wakfs, the rule of decision in cases where the parties are Muslims shall be the Muslim Personal Law (Shariat).
TERMINOLOGY:
  - Shariat :: The body of Islamic law derived from the Quran and the traditions of the Prophet Muhammad.
